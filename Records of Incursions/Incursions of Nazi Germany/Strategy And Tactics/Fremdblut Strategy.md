The Fremdblut Strategy was an attempt at achieving military successes with the help of demonic [[Incursion]]s. It's central idea was that the blood tithe demanded for services by the demonic patron of the Nazis could be payed using the blood of their enemies rather than their own.

# Tactical Overview 
While the strategy remained severely effective due to its occult nature, it does become easy to understand in retrospect.
It begins with the choice of offering. An civilian settlement behind the enemy front is chosen.
You rapidly move forward with a small unit of armoured infantry securing your rear as you detach from the own front.
You must break into the settlement, making sure to leave as many civilians alive as possible.
In a central location, the ritual to alert the [[Demons]] is performed and thus a two way portal opened. Most likely, the unit initialising the ritual will be dead within the hour.

If now, you pressure another front, it creates a dilemma for the enemy as troops are both needed to defend their civilian population and the warfront.
Based on historical example, the amount of demons and their reach from the portal increases over time. Thus, the french and italian surrendered soon after the emplacement under the condition that they would be allowed to tend to it. Much of the polish front was lost before their defeat, a fact the Nazi leadership declared to simply be "the price of resistance"

# Risks
As portals can only be closed from the side which has opened them, this strategy will almost always leave an open portal within the conquered territories. The Nazis seem to have known of that fact. Thought their source is unknown being presumed to be a Demonic Patron.
