# Blutstein
is a term of german origin, its meaning encompasses something along the lines of “stone of blood”, “stone with the qualities of blood” or “stone made from blood”. The widely accepted English term is Bloodcrete, with the technical term Sanguinite recently introduced.

Blutstein is the result of an extradimensional phenomenon introduced from the Plain Of Punishment (Colloquially Named Hell) into our world by the Verdun Incursion of 1940.
There is only sparse evidence of Blutstein occurring on planet earth before this event, all of its occurrences have been heavily contested before the incursion and most remain so now.
The process of Blutstein creation begins with the spilling of demonic blood. It rapidly undergoes a metamorphosis into undifferentiated tissue before it either differentiates into functional tissue or hardens into Blutstein.
During the initial process, Demonic Blood undergoes changes so aggressive that the waste-heat it produces causes it to boil while its fibre forms, causing the bone.- of bread-like structure common to Blutstein.

Blutstein is an uncannily hard but brittle material. Resisting compressive force well but breaking easily under shear or tensile forces, its properties mimic concrete in kind. It is however at least three times as sturdy to compressive forces than concrete, making it an exceptional replacement.
