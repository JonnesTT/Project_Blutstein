Bled by betrayal, many fell to the pits of flesh for our taking. 
The refusal to die, I have seen that a hundred times, and a hundred times I thought it queer. 
Left bloodless the flesh wavers, it's time cut short.
But this one, this one lived and he fed upon us.

Our blood he took, our claim to fuel being. New strength had found him then, new flesh had grown in him. He took my rat, just one devoured the others ran to take him out. 
Above fools shouted. They ran and rained upon him their shard of cold bone but it was too late. He had found our home.

Knowing them, now, The Wretch had been doomed, yet I don't understand his nature. 
Bearing no claw and teeth too weak the tore through the [[Spawning Pits]] of his arrival.
Weapon of bone heeded his call, his will drove all in his wake.
He had seen the plains, nearly spoiled the great plan.
Tore a wound into it still before we spat him out.
And that alone, not to suffer his wrath may have doomed the order.
